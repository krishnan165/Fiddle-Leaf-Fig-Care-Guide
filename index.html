Fiddle Leaf Fig Care Guide

Soil for Fiddle Leaf Fig

Well-draining, aerated soil with added perlite.

Fiddle Leaf Figs prefer a well-draining soil mix with good aeration, often achieved by adding perlite.

Watering

Allow the soil to dry slightly between waterings.

Water the plant when the top inch of the soil feels dry to the touch, avoiding overwatering.

Lighting

Bright, indirect light; avoid direct sunlight.

Place the Fiddle Leaf Fig in a location with bright, indirect light and protect it from direct sunlight.

Benefits

Large, attractive, fiddle-shaped leaves.

The distinctive foliage of the Fiddle Leaf Fig adds a stylish and tropical touch to your indoor space.

Effective air purifier.

Fiddle Leaf Figs help improve indoor air quality by filtering out pollutants.

Non-Benefits

Sensitive to overwatering; root rot can occur if the soil remains consistently wet.
